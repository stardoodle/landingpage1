Course Syllabus
Basics of Space Flight
By Prof. Saptarshi Kolay  ·  IIT Roorkee  ·  4 Weeks · Self-Paced
⬇ Download PDF
Course Objective
To help students understand the fundamentals of space flight — from the physics of the space environment and orbital mechanics to spacecraft systems and mission operations — and apply this knowledge to real-world aerospace problems.
Course Units
UNIT I
The Space Environment
Week 1 · ~1h 24m
Introduction to space flight and its history — Structure of the solar system — The space environment: vacuum, temperature extremes — Microgravity and its effects on materials and humans — Radiation types in space and spacecraft shielding — Overview of major space agencies and missions.
UNIT II
Orbital Mechanics & Trajectories
Week 2 · ~1h 27m
Kepler's laws of planetary motion — Types of orbits: LEO, MEO, GEO, HEO — Orbital velocity and escape velocity — Hohmann transfer orbits — Gravity-assist (slingshot) maneuvers — Interplanetary trajectory basics — Launch windows and delta-v budgets.
UNIT III
Spacecraft Systems
Week 3 · ~1h 19m
Propulsion systems: chemical, electric, and cold-gas — Power systems: solar panels and RTGs — Thermal control: passive and active methods — Attitude determination and control systems (ADCS) — Onboard communication and telemetry — Command and data handling systems.
UNIT IV
Mission Operations & Science
Week 4 · ~1h 52m
Mission design process and trade-offs — Launch vehicle selection and payload integration — Ground station operations and tracking — Interplanetary mission planning — Re-entry and landing systems — Case study: Chandrayaan-3 mission walkthrough — Future of space exploration.

Learning Outcomes
Unit Upon completion, the learner will be able to
UNIT I Explain the structure of the solar system, describe the space environment, and identify how microgravity and radiation affect spacecraft and crew.
UNIT II Apply Kepler's laws to describe orbital motion, differentiate between orbit types, and explain how gravity-assist maneuvers and Hohmann transfers work.
UNIT III Identify the major subsystems of a spacecraft, explain the function of each, and evaluate basic engineering trade-offs in spacecraft design.
UNIT IV Describe the end-to-end process of a space mission, explain interplanetary trajectory planning, and analyse a real mission using Chandrayaan-3 as a case study.
